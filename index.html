🌳 Tree Stump Removal — Lead Sales Scripts

Goal: Get verbal agreement to a 1-week free trial. Route their number. Do NOT discuss pricing.
Single close: “Can I route calls to your number for the next week, no charge?”
Pre-Call Prep

Before You Dial

Owner’s first name confirmed
City / market for their page
Keyword phrase the page ranks for (e.g. “stump removal [city]”)
Competing companies in that market
Opening — Pattern Interrupt

First Words Do not open with “Is this a good time?”

“[Name], this is [Your Name]. Quick question — you still doing stump removal work out of [city]?”

Wait for “yeah” or “who is this” — either is fine.

“Good. I own a website that’s ranking on the first page of Google for stump removal in [city]. It’s been generating phone calls, and right now those calls aren’t going anywhere. I wanted to see if you’d want them before I try somebody else.”

Stop. Let them respond. Do not fill the silence.

If They Ask “What Are You Selling?”

Clarification Response

“Fair question. I’m not selling you a subscription or a software package. I own a website — I built it, I rank it, the calls come from it. I’m looking for one company in [city] to receive those calls. Right now I’m doing a free trial — you get the calls for a week, you decide if they’re worth anything. That’s it.”
Discovery — SPIN Questions (pick 2–3)

Uncover Pain — Do Not Pitch Yet

Situation:

“How’s work coming in right now — word of mouth, referrals, or are you running ads?”

If referrals only → open to new channel. If ads → they already understand lead buying.

Problem:

“What’s the biggest headache with how you’re getting jobs right now — inconsistency, slow months, job size — what is it?”

If “we’re busy” → use Objection 1 below. If they name real pain → use it in the trial pitch.

Implication (only if they named a pain):

“When you have a slow month, does that hit you on the labor side — are you scrambling to keep guys busy, or is it more of a cash flow thing?”

Let them feel it. Do not rush to solve it.

Challenger Teach — Reframe Aggregators

60-Second Teach Moment

“You’ve probably tried Angi or HomeAdvisor at some point — most guys in your trade have. The problem with those isn’t the leads themselves, it’s that the same lead goes to five or six contractors at the same time. You’re not selling your work anymore, you’re racing to answer the phone first and then bidding against guys who’ll do it for less.

What I do is different. I own the website. There’s no marketplace. When someone types ‘stump removal [city]’ into Google and they find my page, they call one number — yours. They’ve never seen another contractor’s name. You’re not competing with anybody.”

Brief pause. Watch for a reaction.

Trial Offer

The Ask

“Here’s what I’m proposing. For the next week, I route those calls straight to your number, no charge. You pick up, you talk to the people, you see if they’re real jobs in your area. If they’re garbage, tell me at the end of the week and we’re done — I won’t even ask why. If they’re solid leads and you’re booking jobs off them, we can talk about what that’s worth to you. Does that sound like a waste of a week?”

Let them answer. If “what’s the catch” → Objection 4 below.

Objection Handlers
Acknowledge“That’s actually a good problem to have — a lot of guys I talk to are starving for work right now.”
Explore“Is that busy meaning you’re fully booked for weeks, or more like you’ve got enough to keep the crew running but it’s not predictable?”
Respond“The reason I still bring this up even when guys are busy is that this source is exclusive and tied to one market. Once I place someone in [city], I don’t offer it to anyone else. So if things slow down in six weeks — and they usually do seasonally — you’d have a lead source already proven and ready. The trial doesn’t cost you anything to run while you’re busy.”
Confirm“Does that make sense to keep in your back pocket?”
Acknowledge“Yeah, I hear that a lot. Most of those services are garbage, and they’ve earned that reputation.”
Explore“Was the problem mostly that leads were shared with other guys, or were the jobs just not in your area?”
Respond“Both of those are baked-in problems with aggregators — they’re selling the same lead to multiple people because that’s their business model. What I’m describing isn’t a marketplace. I own the site, I control the traffic, and I place one company per market. The trial is specifically so you can verify that for yourself.”
Confirm“Would a week of live calls be enough for you to tell the difference?”
Acknowledge“That’s exactly the right question — and honestly, I can’t prove it to you on this call.”
Explore“What would a qualified lead look like for you — someone in [city] with a stump on their property and money to pay for it, or is there more to it?”
Respond“The only way to actually verify it is to take the calls yourself. That’s why the trial exists. I’m not asking you to pay for something you can’t evaluate. Run it for a week, keep notes on who calls and what they need.”
Confirm“Does that feel like a fair way to check it out?”
Acknowledge“Good instinct to ask — I would too.”
Explore“What are you most skeptical about?”
Respond“The trial is genuinely free. No credit card, no contract, no auto-convert to billing. After the week, if you want to continue, we’ll have a conversation about what the leads are worth. If you don’t want to continue, I find someone else. The only ‘catch’ is that your number goes on the site and callers can reach you. That’s it.”
Confirm“Does that change anything for you?”
Acknowledge“Completely fair — I’m not trying to rush you.”
Explore“What’s the part you want to think through? Is it the trial setup itself, or something about how it works?”
Respond“If it’s the trial, there’s nothing financially at stake — it’s a week of phone calls. The only decision right now is whether you want a week of free calls. Can you check with them and let me know by [specific day]?”
Confirm“What day works to get back to me?”
Close

Confirm Trial Agreement

“Perfect. What’s the best number to route calls to — the one you’re on right now, or a different line?”

Confirm the number back to them.

“Got it. I’ll get that set up within 24 hours. I’ll follow up with you in a week — roughly [specific date]. By then you’ll know exactly what came in. Sound good?”

Let them confirm. End the call. Do not keep selling.

Goal: Use their trial numbers to present pricing, handle objections, and close on an ongoing arrangement.
Single close: “Let’s keep your number on the site and set up a billing cycle.”
Pre-Call Prep

Before You Dial

Calls routed to them this week (your routing data = n)
Pain they named on Call 1
Their city / market
Your finder’s fee: f = 10%

You’ll collect c (close rate) and p (avg ticket) from them on the call. Don’t name numbers first.

Warm Re-Opener

First Words

“[Name], it’s [Your Name] — I set up the lead routing for your stump removal work in [city] last week. How’d those calls treat you?”

Wait. Let them lead. This tells you immediately whether calls were good, bad, or nonexistent.

If Calls Were Slow or Mixed

Diagnose Before Pitching

“I appreciate you telling me. What happened — were they wrong area, wrong job type, not ready to book, or just not many calls?”

⚠️ If calls were genuinely bad quality, be straight: “That’s not what I want you paying for either. Let me look at the traffic data and come back to you.” Do not close on bad-quality leads.

Discovery — Close Rate & Revenue

Get Their Numbers — Do Not Suggest Amounts

“Walk me through the numbers real quick. How many calls came in this week?”

Record their answer. Use their number as n if it differs from your routing data.

“Out of those, how many turned into actual booked jobs?”

This is c — their close rate. Example: 3 calls, 2 booked = 67%.

“And on a typical stump removal job — what’s an average ticket for you? Not the biggest job, just a normal one.”

This is p. Let them name it. Do not suggest a number.

Pricing Formula
Price = p × n × c × f
p = avg profit per job (they name it) n = leads delivered this period c = close rate (jobs ÷ calls) f = 10% finder’s fee

Walk the Math Out Loud With Their Numbers

“Okay, so here’s how I think about what this is worth. It’s pretty simple math — your number, not mine.

You told me you average about [p] dollars a job. You booked [c count] out of [n] calls this week — that’s roughly [c%] close rate. If that holds over a month, you’re looking at roughly [n×4] calls a month, [n×4×c] jobs, at [p] per job — that’s [monthly revenue] in additional revenue from this one source.

The fee I charge is 10% of that. So on this week alone, we’re talking [p × n × c × 0.10] dollars.”

Let that land. Don’t immediately justify it. Watch their reaction.

Anchor 10% as Below-Market — Say This Proactively

Frame Before They Object

“I’ll tell you why it’s 10% and not more. Most lead generation services charge 15 to 20% when they’re doing performance-based pricing — and they charge you even when leads don’t convert. I’m early in building this out. I’d rather prove it works and earn your trust than squeeze you on the first contract. If I’m delivering consistent, quality calls, I’d rather have you as a long-term client than nickel-and-dime you on the rate.”
Value Frame

Say the Value Equation Out Loud

“The way I think about what you’re actually getting: you’re getting calls from people in your area who searched specifically for stump removal and found your number. You didn’t run an ad, you didn’t pay for a click, you didn’t compete with five other guys for the same lead. You just answered the phone.

The only effort on your end is picking up and booking the job. The territory is yours — I’m not placing another stump removal company in [city] while you’re on this. And if the calls stop being quality, you tell me and we adjust.”
Objection Handlers
Acknowledge“Fair enough — let’s look at the actual math instead of arguing about a percentage.”
Explore“If you’re booking [c] jobs a week off this at [p] each, what would you need to see to feel like the fee was justified?”
Respond“You’re paying 10% on revenue you didn’t have before. Your labor costs, your equipment, your truck — those are the same. This is additional margin. 90% of something is better than 100% of nothing. And this is below what you’d pay running Google Ads yourself.”
Confirm“Does the math work for you when you look at it that way?”
Acknowledge“That’s a legit concern — you don’t want a fixed cost if the calls dry up.”
Explore“Are you worried about paying a retainer when nothing comes in, or is it more about the flexibility to stop if things change?”
Respond“The fee is calculated on actual calls delivered and jobs booked. The formula is p × n × c × f. If n is zero, the fee is zero. You’re not betting on a fixed subscription — you’re paying for performance.”
Confirm“Does that address the risk you were thinking about?”
Acknowledge“I appreciate you saying that directly — I’d rather negotiate openly than have you feel like you got a bad deal.”
Explore“What rate were you thinking, and is this about the percentage or more about wanting a cap on the total?”
Respond“I’m at 10% because I want the arrangement to be sustainable for you. If call volume consistently exceeds what you’re getting now — say, 20+ calls a month — let’s revisit together. I’d rather grow this with you than win the negotiation and lose the client.”
Confirm“Can we commit to 10% for the first 60 days and then have that conversation with real data?”
Acknowledge“Of course — this is a business decision, not something you need to decide in the next two minutes.”
Explore“What’s the part you want to think through? Is it the rate, the commitment structure, or something else?”
Respond“Here’s what I’d ask you to bring to that conversation: the trial data you already have. [n] calls, [c booked], [p×c×n] in jobs. That’s a week of real results — not a pitch deck.” ⚠️ Only use scarcity if it is real.
Confirm“When can you two sit down and look at this? I want to give you space but also know when to follow up.”
Acknowledge“Makes sense — one week isn’t a full picture.”
Explore“How many weeks do you feel like you’d need to see before you felt confident?”
Respond“I can extend the trial another week at no charge. What I’d ask in return is that if the calls stay consistent, we agree today on the pricing structure — so we’re not re-negotiating after the second trial.”
Confirm“Does that feel fair to both of us?”
Close

Assumptive Close — When Buying Signals Are Present

“Based on everything — the calls you got this week, the numbers we just walked through — does it make sense to keep your number on the site and move this from a trial to an ongoing arrangement?”

If yes:

“Good. Here’s what happens next: your number stays routed as-is, nothing changes on your end. I’ll put together a simple invoice based on the calls and jobs for the first billing period. I’ll send it to you at [email or text]. You review it, if the numbers match what you tracked, you pay it. If something doesn’t add up, you call me first. Sound workable?”
Confirm Next Steps — Leave No Ambiguity

Summarize Verbally

“Let me summarize what we agreed to:

— Your number stays on the site starting now
— Fee is 10% of p × n × c — calculated on actual calls delivered and jobs booked
— First invoice covers [this week / the month], I’ll send it to [email/text] by [specific date]
— Check-in every 4 weeks to review call volume and quality
— Either of us can end the arrangement with one week’s notice

Does that cover it?”

Get verbal confirmation. Then:

“Good. I’ll send you a short summary of what we agreed to by text / email so you’ve got it in writing. Thanks for running the trial — talk soon.”
